<p>You’d be forgiven for&nbsp;thinking that your initial shortlist of&nbsp;SAM or<strong>&nbsp;<a href="https://www.certero.com/itam/">ITAM tools</a></strong>&nbsp;should be formed&nbsp;purely&nbsp;on functionality.&nbsp; After all, you need a tool that covers the right platforms, collects the right data, has a nice big software recognition library&nbsp;and&nbsp;supports licensing for the vendors you care about most.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>If the tool can’t support&nbsp;the&nbsp;vision for what your SAM or ITAM program is trying to achieve, then it’s a non-starter.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>But what about the architecture of potential&nbsp;SAM&nbsp;tools and platforms?&nbsp; How much consideration&nbsp;should&nbsp;you put into how the solutions on your shortlist are&nbsp;actually&nbsp;built&nbsp;and deployed?&nbsp; And what does it matter anyway?&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Let’s look at a few reasons why&nbsp;the&nbsp;architecture&nbsp;of SAM and ITAM tools&nbsp;is perhaps more important than it first appears.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Disparate products make for data mess&nbsp;</strong><strong>&amp;</strong><strong>&nbsp;data loss&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>Many ITAM and SAM&nbsp;vendors’ offerings&nbsp;have grown&nbsp;over the years&nbsp;by acquisition and are now more a collection of ‘integrated’&nbsp;(sometimes in the loosest sense)&nbsp;products rather than a single&nbsp;cohesive&nbsp;platform.&nbsp; For example, the discovery and inventory module might&nbsp;have a&nbsp;completely separate&nbsp;interface and codebase to&nbsp;the license management, workflow automation or analytics capabilities.&nbsp;&nbsp;Likewise, there might not even be a single database for the products, with data residing in different places and formats, even though the products are presented as an ‘integrated solution’.&nbsp;</p>



<p>Why does this matter?&nbsp;&nbsp;For a number of reasons.&nbsp; First, SAM and ITAM are not one-trick ponies; success in these programs often requires a range of tools to be brought to bear.&nbsp; If those tools are not working together seamlessly, it makes your life much more difficult.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Similarly, if you want&nbsp;to&nbsp;both collect and utilize inventory data from across the network, then you need a solution that not only collects the right data (and by that, we’re not just talking Windows PCs and Servers, but all platforms and all IP-addressable devices) but maintains that data in a single source that can be used to satisfy the needs of a wide range of both Software Asset Management&nbsp;<em>and</em>&nbsp;IT Asset Management use cases. Disparate solutions struggle to support the full range of use cases as they are often restrictive on what data is&nbsp;collected, retained and made available to&nbsp;multiple&nbsp;stakeholders&nbsp;through different parts of the product portfolio.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>As such, the architecture of the solutions can have a direct impact on their ability to support more complex and diverse use cases.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong><em>The answer:</em></strong>&nbsp;Think about a true platform where data collection, processing, storage and availability&nbsp;are all&nbsp;central to the foundation of the solution.&nbsp;&nbsp;Identify&nbsp;SAM and ITAM&nbsp;platforms where&nbsp;multiple use cases can&nbsp;readily&nbsp;be supported from a single&nbsp;data&nbsp;source without the need to setup and maintain complicated integrations,&nbsp;and where data critical to some stakeholders outside SAM isn’t discarded out of hand.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Time to get up&nbsp;</strong><strong>&amp;</strong><strong>&nbsp;running&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>If you’ve got the budget sign-off and you’re ready to go, the last thing you want to do is wait for months to get your chosen solution up and running. Yet this is exactly what happens in many cases, whether it’s due to having to wait for internal teams to stand up servers or create permissions for&nbsp;services to be set up, or waiting on availability from the vendor’s Professional Services team to&nbsp;either&nbsp;come on-site and install the necessary technology&nbsp;or remotely provision cloud resources.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>It’s&nbsp;worse when the&nbsp;technology itself requires multiple application and database installs.&nbsp; And even worse still when getting different parts of the same vendor’s product portfolio to talk to each other takes complicated bespoke integration work.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Suddenly that Software Asset Management project you got signed-off&nbsp;(and paid for)&nbsp;in&nbsp;March&nbsp;still isn’t&nbsp;delivering any value&nbsp;in&nbsp;Summer.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong><em>The answer:</em></strong>&nbsp;Look for a&nbsp;vendor and&nbsp;platform that takes away as much of the onboarding pain as possible, either by taking advantage of&nbsp;true&nbsp;SaaS architecture to almost completely bypass the need to stand up internal resources&nbsp;(and a vendor that can provision servers and instances in minutes, not weeks)&nbsp;or using that same architecture to minimize the complexity of on-premises installs and configuration.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Whether you use it in the cloud or on-premises, a true SaaS architecture will ensure your SAM or ITAM program is up and running in record time.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Time to deliver value&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>Even when you’re installed, you could still be a long way from delivering real value back to the organization.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;SAM and ITAM solutions that depend almost exclusively on client agents to provide inventory can be especially prone to slow time to value (TTV).&nbsp;</p>



<p>Configuring and preparing agents for deployment can be an arduous&nbsp;process, especially in&nbsp;data center scenarios or&nbsp;environments where multiple customers are ‘sharing’ cloud instances.&nbsp;&nbsp;Even&nbsp;when&nbsp;they are&nbsp;ready, agents can only be deployed to&nbsp;the machines already known to the SAM or ITAM teams (remember Rumsfeld’s “unknown unknowns” speech?).&nbsp;&nbsp;So&nbsp;if the tools lacks a strong discovery capability, your chances of achieving full coverage of the estate are small.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>And getting agents deployed to sensitive parts of the IT estate, such as data centers, can be near-impossible.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Once agents are deployed, it’s quite common to then face challenges on getting them reporting back to the inventory server, or keeping deployed agents up-to-date.&nbsp;</p>



<p>All of which means that&nbsp;it can take a long time to build an accurate view of the IT estate and start driving value to the business.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong><em>The answer:</em></strong>&nbsp;Look for a platform that provides the utmost flexibility.&nbsp; Whether that’s in the ability to use effective alternatives to deploying client agents or to make it easier to collect and process&nbsp;agent-generated&nbsp;inventory data.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>Also look to address those unknown unknowns with a solution that doesn’t just inventory what you already know about, but helps you&nbsp;proactively&nbsp;discover&nbsp;new or previously-unrecorded devices (again, not just Windows PCs and servers)&nbsp;across&nbsp;the network.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Increasing the scope of the SAM or ITAM program&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>The chances are that, as you deliver success to the organization, you’ll want to expand the scope of your ITAM or SAM program.&nbsp; Perhaps you’ll want to start tracking cloud spend. Or to bring mobile devices into the program.&nbsp;&nbsp;Or start optimizing software for a new vendor.&nbsp;Perhaps you’ll want to start performing a deeper analysis of the inventory, entitlement or spend data.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>With some&nbsp;solutions, adding&nbsp;new functionality can be a similar to experience to your initial implementation, thanks to new applications having to be installed, configured and connected to the existing parts of the portfolio you’re already using.&nbsp;&nbsp;This creates delay and cost that is the enemy of making&nbsp;smooth&nbsp;progress.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>You also might conceivably want to&nbsp;increase the number of devices being inventoried and supported (through acquisition, inclusion of additional business units, new platforms etc.).&nbsp; If you’re running on-premises, you’ll need to think ahead and make sure that the not only can the solution process the required volume of data in a reasonable time (some solutions effectively lock-out users for hours at a time while processing inventory data), but that the hardware is also up to the task.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong><em>The answer:</em></strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;It makes much more sense to have access to new functionality the instant&nbsp;you want it.&nbsp; The only way to achieve that is through a platform approach where the functionality is already in place, just waiting to be activated.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>For organizations that deploy in the cloud, scaling up the solution to support the efficient processing of additional data&nbsp;should be&nbsp;much less of an issue, as the vendor can quickly upgrade the cloud resources to provide additional power and storage.&nbsp;</p>



<p><strong>Conclusion:&nbsp;</strong><strong>So how important is architecture?&nbsp;</strong>&nbsp;</p>



<p>The functionality of a SAM&nbsp;or ITAM&nbsp;tool is&nbsp;unquestionably&nbsp;very important.&nbsp; If you need to optimize your Oracle data center licensing and the candidate tool doesn’t support Oracle licensing or&nbsp;inventorying the data center, it’s an immediate red flag.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>



<p>But assuming the tool vendor claims it can support the scenario, it then becomes critical to understand&nbsp;more around the general experience of buying, onboarding, using and supporting their solution in the longer term.&nbsp; That’s where architecture can make a real difference, in terms of cost, functionality and the sanity of the teams trying to use the solution.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
